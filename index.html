Iconic, beautiful, and jaw dropping — words can never be enough to describe the beauty of this landmark of eternal love. Taj Mahal in Agra, one of the most-admired seven wonders in the world is a classic tale of declaration of love by a Mughal Emperor Shahjahan for his wife, Mumtaz. The artistic masterpiece was built by Shahjahan after the demise of his wife during child birth, as an apparent token of love in her memory.The mausoleum in Agra, Taj Mahal, is India’s most famous monument and a shrine of timeless love. Built between 1632 and 1653 AD by Shahjahan, the 5th Mughal Emperor of the Mughal Dynasty, it took 21 years and 32 crore rupees to come up with this epitome of true love and passion. Its pristine white marble shines brilliantly through the sun rays providing for a visual masterpiece. Taj Mahal was declared as UNESCO world heritage site in 1983 and has been a winner of several laurels ever since.
Sinhagad is approximately 30km from Pune. After 40 minutes my stop arrived. The bus dropped me at Donje phata. I asked for the route to the fort at a shop on the Y junction. The bus took right turn and I had to take a left road. I thought the fort is nearby. I was just walking and walking, seeing people who were going by their private vehicles. There was no one except me walking on the road. While I was walking a bus came from the direction of the fort, then the image was clear. Bus number 50 goes to the base. There were many adventure parks on the way. Again there came a Y junction. Left way is for them who don’t want to trek and just reach the fort by private vehicles. Parking facilities are available on the top. The right way is for trekkers. I confirmed the path and again started walking. There are some hotels if someone wants to have breakfast there. Slowly slowly the fort covered behind the thin layer of clouds came into sight. I almost walked for 45 minutes. There is a huge tower on the fort, hence there is a network on the phone all the time. After a long walk, I reached the base, lemonade seller is the landmark. From there we have to take the right path. I rested for 5-10 minutes over there, had some biscuits. There were many private vehicles parked over there. People usually start the trek prior to sunrise and return before noon, but it was not possible for me. I then started ascending. The path is clear. While I was going to the fort there were many groups who were on their way back to the home.The fort has two Darwaza. The one I entered from was Pune Darwaza and the other at the rear end of the fort was Kalyan Darwaza. The Kalyan Darwaza was also in a very good condition. This entrance is often used by trekkers.To the right of Pune Darwaza, there is KHANDKADA. There are bastion and fortification over there. Parking area can be seen from there. Also, we can see Kalyan Darwaza from there.It was going to Shanivar Wada. I boarded the bus happily. It was a great day for me because I completed a solo trek. Reached Shanivar Wada and from there boarded the bus to Hadapsar. Reached home by 6:30pm.
